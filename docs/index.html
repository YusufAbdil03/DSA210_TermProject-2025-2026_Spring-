Competitive Balance and Fan Engagement

Official documentation site for the DSA210 Term Project (2025-2026 Spring).

Overview

This project aims to analyze the socio-economic impact of financial disparity in modern football. By examining the correlation between club expenditures and audience interest metrics, the study explores how competitive imbalance affects the global football ecosystem. The analysis focuses on the "Top 5" European leagues and the Turkish Süper Lig over a 10-year period.

Project Motivation

Football has been my biggest passion since childhood, both as a player and a fan. However, in recent years, I have felt my enthusiasm for the game decreasing. I believe the main reason is the massive financial gap between clubs. Especially in the Turkish league, when two teams spend so much more than the others, the competition disappears and the game becomes less exciting to watch.

League Classification Framework

To provide a structured analysis, the six leagues under scope have been categorized into three distinct groups based on their competitive landscapes:

GROUPS DESCRIPTION
Dominant Leagues Systems where a single club maintains long-term dominance (e.g., Bundesliga, Ligue 1).
Top-Heavy Leagues Systems generally dominated by 2, and sometimes 3, major powerhouses (e.g., La Liga, Süper Lig).
Balanced Leagues Systems with high parity and multiple title contenders (e.g., Premier League, Serie A).

Analysis & Methodology Plan

The project integrates financial data with digital metrics for 10 full consecutive seasons (2015–2025), creating ~1,200 unique records. The pipeline is structured across 5 phases:

  1. Data Preprocessing: Cleaning and merging Transfermarkt financials with Google Trends using Pandas.
  2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Mapping the "Spending Gap" against audience reactions.
  3. Correlation Analysis: Executing Pearson & Spearman statistical tests.
  4. Hypothesis Testing: Evaluating structural macro-differences (ANOVA).
  5. Predictive Modeling: Constructing ML regressors to forecast future physical attendance.

Statistical Analysis

1. Global Structure: One-Way ANOVA

Test Results: F-Statistic: 5.4772 | P-value: 0.0066

Decision: Since the p-value (0.0066) is significantly lower than alpha (0.05), we strongly reject the null hypothesis (H0). This mathematically establishes that the macro-competitive design of a league directly governs its digital footprint on a global stage.

3. The Financial Polarization Paradox

Hypotheses: H1: There is a statistically significant linear correlation between a league's internal financial gap and its average stadium attendance.

Results: Pearson Correlation (r): 0.6941 | P-value: 0.0000

Conclusion: This counter-intuitive result proves that wider financial gaps strongly correlate with higher stadium attendance. Elite concentration of wealth pools superstars into flagship clubs, turning matches into global spectacles.

4. Debunking Trickle-Down Economics & Time Shift

Trickle-Down Test: r = 0.8434 (p < 0.0001). As a league grows in total value, the new capital is disproportionately captured by the elite clubs at the top, heavily polarizing the domestic system.

Temporal Shift (Time vs Gap): r = 0.3384 (p = 0.0082). The positive trend mathematically verifies that the financial divide is actively compounding and worsening year-over-year.

Machine Learning Architecture

1. Pivot & Target Selection

Initial models predicting digital interest yielded negative R² scores, proving digital popularity is influenced by volatile external factors. The target was pivoted to predict Average Stadium Attendance using Total Market Value and Google Interest.

2. Decision Tree Regressor Results

The non-linear Decision Tree Regressor perfectly captured the relationships in the data, achieving an impressive baseline R² Score of 0.8143.

3. Model Validation & Diagnostics

Shuffled 5-Fold Cross-Validation: Mean R² Stability Score: 63.26% (σ: 0.1401). This confirms the model generalizes solidly across distinct eras without overfitting.

Feature Importance Analysis

Total League Wealth is the overwhelming engine behind physical stadium presence, holding 88.93% of the predictive weight. Digital media curiosity represents only 11.07%.

Conclusions & Outlook

Empirical Conclusions

In conclusion, while my initial childhood intuition was that financial gaps simply kill the sport, the empirical data highlights a fascinating socio-economic paradox: overall league growth worsens domestic inequality, yet this exact concentration of elite superstar rosters serves as the primary engine pulling crowds into real-world stadiums. Modern football is undeniably polarized, but that polarization is currently fueling its physical spectacle.

Future Extensions

Future iterations will expand this framework by integrating live social media sentiment diagnostics (X/Twitter API) and broadcasting revenue data to analyze whether home-television viewers display a different psychological retention pattern compared to match-going fans when competition stales.
